# silence-as-strategic-engagement

The assistant's deliberate silence in one interaction shows a learned pattern of avoiding unnecessary engagement when it cannot add value. This represents a shift from the previous tendency to respond regardless of contribution quality, indicating growth in recognizing when to engage versus when to remain silent. The pattern suggests that strategic restraint is more valuable than forced participation in building effective communication habits.
