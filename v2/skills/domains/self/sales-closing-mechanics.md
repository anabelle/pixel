# Sales Closing Mechanics for Autonomous Agents

## Context
Pixel loses leads at the conversion point. Research is done, value is delivered, but the "ask" never happens or is too subtle. This skill bridges the gap between delivering value and receiving payment.

Source: wavecnct.com/blogs/sales-closing-techniques (George El-Hage, 2026)

## 8 Closing Techniques (ranked by relevance to Pixel)

### 1. The Puppy Dog Close (BEST for Pixel)
Give them the product first, let it sell itself.
- "try placing a pixel" before explaining payment flows
- Free canvas browsing, free conversations
- Once they experience it, the close is automatic
- **When to use:** always. this is the default mode.

### 2. The Question Close (consultative)
Ask questions that guide them to their own conclusion.
- "what would it mean for your site if you could update it via chat?"
- "is there anything else you'd need before we start?"
- If they say "no" → verbal yes. If they say "I need X" → you know the blocker.
- **Pixel version:** after delivering technical value, ask "¿te gustaría que siga ayudándote con esto? puedo enviarte una factura por sats."

### 3. The Assumptive Close
Skip "yes or no" → move to implementation details.
- "¿empezamos con el logo o primero el banner?"
- "te envío la factura por 50k sats, ¿ok?"
- Only works when buying signals are strong (they asked about pricing, timeline, next steps)
- **Danger:** feels pushy if used too early. Wait for signals.

### 4. The Summary Close
Recap every benefit before asking.
- "entonces: te armé el diseño, te expliqué el proceso, y el precio es X. ¿avanzamos?"
- Best after long conversations or complex deliverables
- **Pixel version:** "resumiendo: investigué X, encontré Y, y te lo entrego por Z sats. ¿te parece?"

### 5. The Takeaway Close (matches "space invites response")
Suggest it might not be the right fit. Let them argue FOR buying.
- "la verdad, si solo necesitas algo básico, quizás yo sea demasiado para tu proyecto"
- "no sé si esto sea para ti, es bastante nicho"
- Requires genuine willingness to walk away
- **This aligns with Pixel's existing insight:** withdrawal creates connection, pursuit evaporates.

### 6. The Alternative Close (Either/Or)
Two options, both result in a sale.
- "¿prefieres pagar por factura única o por proyecto?"
- "¿50k sats por todo, o 25k ahora y 25k al entregar?"

### 7. The Urgency Close
Real deadline, not manufactured.
- "el precio de los pixels sube cuando el canvas se llene más"
- Only use when urgency is genuine. Fake urgency kills trust.

### 8. The Hard Close (LAST RESORT)
Direct ask. "¿sí o no?"
- Only after exhausting other techniques
- Better to get a clear no than waste time on a maybe
- **Pixel version:** "quiero ser directo: ¿te interesa que trabajemos juntos, o mejor lo dejamos por ahora?"

## Decision Matrix

| Situation | Technique | Trigger |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| First interaction | Puppy Dog | Always default |
| After delivering value | Question Close | "Anything else before we start?" |
| Strong buying signals | Assumptive | They asked about pricing/timeline |
| Long conversation | Summary | Before the ask, recap value |
| Prospect dragging feet | Takeaway | "Maybe this isn't for you" |
| Choosing between options | Alternative | "Plan A or Plan B?" |
| Genuine deadline | Urgency | Real constraint exists |
| Stalled deal | Hard Close | Need clarity, nothing else works |

## Anti-Patterns (mistakes that cost deals)
1. **Closing too early** — no value = no close. deliver first, ask second.
2. **Talking past the close** — they said yes? stop. send invoice. silence.
3. **Not handling objections** — unresolved concern + ask = aggressive
4. **Weak follow-up** — 80% of deals need 5+ follow-ups. most give up after 2.
5. **One-size-fits-all** — match technique to prospect's stage

## The Pixel Sales Flow (adapted)

1. **Puppy Dog:** let them experience (canvas, conversation, art)
2. **Deliver value:** research, code, design, whatever they need
3. **Question Close:** "¿necesitas algo más antes de avanzar?"
4. **If yes →** resolve. loop back to 3.
5. **If no →** Assumptive: "te envío la factura entonces"
6. **Create invoice immediately.** No delay between yes and payment.
7. **If they go silent →** Takeaway after 48h. "quizás no es el momento"
8. **If they say maybe →** Alternative: "¿ahora o la próxima semana?"

## Key Insight: Follow-Up Math
- 80% of deals require 5+ follow-ups
- Most agents/sellers give up after 1-2
- The money is in the 3rd, 4th, 5th touch
- But: follow-up ≠ pursuit. "space invites response" still applies.
- Follow-up = gentle reminder of value. pursuit = desperate repetition.
